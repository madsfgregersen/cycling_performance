# Geo Park Gran Fondo — 7-Week Training Plan

**Event:** Geo Park — 150 km, ~2,000 m elevation, ~12 hills
**Race day:** Sunday, August 30, 2026
**Plan window:** July 13 – August 30 (7 weeks)

---

## The thesis

Geo Park is a different animal than La Marmotte. Marmotte was sustained alpine climbing — long, steady, aerobic. Geo Park is about **repeatability**: going hard up a hill, recovering on the descent, and doing that twelve times without the last few falling apart.

The aerobic base (8–10k km/year) already covers the distance easily. The limiter on the day will be repeated threshold/VO2 efforts and recovery between them. So this plan biases toward **quality and race-specific efforts** rather than piling on volume.

**Assumptions:** solid base, coming in reasonably fresh, ~8–12 hrs/week across 4–6 days. Intensities are expressed relative to FTP — map them to your own numbers.

---

## Week-by-week

### Week 1 — Jul 13–19 · Build: wake up the top end
Reintroduce structured intensity.
- Threshold: 3×10 min @ 95–100%
- VO2 opener: 5×3 min @ ~115%
- Long endurance: 3–4 hrs Z2 over rolling terrain

*~8–10 hrs*

### Week 2 — Jul 20–26 · Build: threshold volume
Push threshold duration; introduce race-specific over-unders.
- Threshold: 2×20 min @ 95–100%
- Over-unders: 3×[2 min @ 105% / 3 min @ 88–90%]
- Long ride: 4 hrs hitting 4–6 short climbs at tempo/threshold

*~9–11 hrs*

### Week 3 — Jul 27–Aug 2 · Build peak: biggest load
Your hardest week; expect real fatigue by the end.
- Threshold: 3×12 min
- VO2: 5×4 min @ 115%
- Longest ride: 4.5–5 hrs simulating rolling terrain
- Second easy endurance ride

*~10–12 hrs*

### Week 4 — Aug 3–9 · Recovery / absorption
Cut volume 40–50%, mostly easy. This is where the last three weeks turn into fitness.
- One light opener: 3×5 min tempo (keeps legs from going flat)
- Optional FTP re-test at week's end for fresh numbers going into specificity

*~5–6 hrs*

### Week 5 — Aug 10–16 · Specificity: race simulation begins
Everything now points at the event.
- Longer over-unders: 4×[3 min over / 3 min under]
- Hill repeats at event gradient: 6–8×4–6 min @ threshold, recover on descent (the exact race skill)
- 4–4.5 hr ride: go hard on *every* hill, practice recovering between them

*~9–11 hrs*

### Week 6 — Aug 17–23 · Specificity peak + last big ride
Highest-quality week, front-loaded.
- Big race-simulation ride early/mid-week: ~4–5 hrs, hills at goal effort, fueling and pacing exactly as race day
- One shorter sharp session
- Start easing by the weekend

*~8–10 hrs, tapering into Sunday*

### Week 7 — Aug 24–30 · Taper + race
Volume down ~50–60% from peak; keep riding most days but short.
- Openers 2–3 days out: short spin with a few 1–2 min efforts at race intensity
- Easy spin or full rest the day before
- **Race: Sunday, Aug 30**

Priority this week is freshness, sleep, and logistics — not fitness. That's already banked.

---

## Three things that cut across all seven weeks

**Gate hard days on your morning signal, not the calendar.** If readiness is genuinely down, swap an intensity day for endurance and move the quality session later. Matters most in the two build peaks (weeks 3 and 6), where the temptation to push through is highest and so is the cost.

**Rehearse fueling on every long ride.** For a ~5-hour event, practicing 70–90 g carbs/hour now means no surprises on the day. The gut trains like anything else.

**The taper isn't optional.** The fitness is made in weeks 1–6; week 7 just protects it. Cutting volume while keeping a little intensity is what lets it surface.
